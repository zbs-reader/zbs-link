# Salt in the Lantern Glass

The tide should not have reached the seventh bridge. It lapped against the old stone with a patient hiss, as if the drowned district had leaned closer during the night and decided to keep what little remained above the waterline.

Mira crossed with one hand on the lantern cage at her belt, feeling the thin warmth of the ember inside. The glass had fogged from the salt air. Somewhere beyond the ruined colonnades, bells rang with no rhythm she recognized.

> "If the city remembers," her mentor had once said, "it remembers through echoes."

At the bridge's far end, a stairway she had never seen before climbed into a bank of copper mist.
